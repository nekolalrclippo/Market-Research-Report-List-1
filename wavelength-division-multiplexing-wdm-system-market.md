<p><strong>The Wavelength Division Multiplexing (WDM) System Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Coarse Wavelength-division Multiplexing (CWDM)</li><li>Dense Wavelength-division Multiplexing (DWDM).</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Wavelength Division Multiplexing (WDM) systems enhance optical fiber communication by allowing multiple wavelengths to transmit data simultaneously over a single fiber. Coarse Wavelength-division Multiplexing (CWDM) utilizes a wider channel spacing of 20 nm, enabling a smaller number of channels, making it cost-effective for access networks. In contrast, Dense Wavelength-division Multiplexing (DWDM) features closer channel spacing, typically 0.8 nm to 1.6 nm, allowing for many more channels, ideal for high-capacity applications and long-distance communication.</p></p>
